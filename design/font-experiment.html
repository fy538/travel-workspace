Lisbon Dossier
Where old Lisbon still lives
A neighbourhood guide to Alfama and Mouraria

Alfama predates the earthquake of 1755 that levelled most of Lisbon. The Moorish street plan — narrow, winding, built for donkeys and foot traffic — survived, and so did the culture that grew inside it.

The result is the only part of central Lisbon that feels genuinely old rather than restored. The tiles on the walls are chipped. The tascas are dark. The miradouros catch the afternoon light in a way that makes you understand why painters kept coming back.
